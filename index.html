A real Gemma 2 (2B) has a dial for each concept it knows. Drag one and the model leans toward it — or away from it — until you let go and it’s exactly, provably itself again, because nothing was ever written to a weight. Every dial below does that same thing to the model. What changes is only how the dial's direction was found.

what this one shows

Two ways to find a steering direction inside the same model, side by side. SAE feature — Gemma Scope (Google DeepMind) trains a sparse autoencoder to decompose Gemma 2's residual stream into ~16,000 monosemantic directions; pick one and you’re steering a direction the model already uses on its own terms. ActAdd contrast pair — Turner et al., 2023; no autoencoder, no training — the direction is the difference between two activations you chose by writing two sentences. Both are additive: a forward hook adds the direction to one layer's residual stream during generation, and both are reversible at strength 0, because neither ever touches a weight.
